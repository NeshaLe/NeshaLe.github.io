Current legislative regarding cosmetic products


Republic of Serbia:

1. Law on General Use Items (“Official Gazette of the RS”, No. 25/2019-88) – sr.Zakon o predmetima opšte upotrebe („Službeni glasnik RS“, br. 25/2019-88);

2. Rulebook on cosmetic products (“Official Gazette of the RS”, No. 60/2019-4) – sr. Pravilnik o kozmetičkim proizvodima („Službeni glasnik RS“, br. 60/2019-4).


EU:

1. Regulation (EC) No 1223/2009 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 30 November 2009 on cosmetic products (and its amendments)

11th of April 2023.

All cosmetic products that are or intended to be placed on the market in the Republic of Serbia, same as for cosmetics in the European Union, have to comply with certain regulations in order to provide seamless market functioning and a high level of protection of human health. These requirements primarily relate to the cosmetic product safety assessment and preparation of a Cosmetic Product Safety Report (CPSR), as well as to the preparation and maintenance of the mandatory supporting product documentation in the form of Product Information File (PIF). To this end, on the authorities request, for each cosmetic product available on the market manufacturer shell provide the PIF, containing, among the various data, CPSR as a crucial part that should be an indication of product safety.

Since the Regulation in the Republic of Serbia harmonized with the EU Regulation 1223/2009 got implemented on 11th of April 2019, grace period of 4 years was allowed for producers to comply with parts of Regulation regarding the PIF and CPSR. Namely, according to the Article 117 of the harmonized national Law:

“Subjects in the cosmetics business are obligated to comply with the Articles 70. (Safety Assessment) and 72. (PIF) of this Law at the latest four years after its implementation (11.04.2019.).”

Product Information File

The most important set of data that PIF shell contain (please check the exhaustive list of the data set needed for PIF compilation):

1. Description of the cosmetic product which enables clear and unambiguous connection of the cosmetic product with the appropriate PIF;
2. Cosmetic Product Safety Report;
3. Description of the production process and certificate/statement on compliance with good manufacturing practice (GMP);
4. Proof of the effect claimed for the cosmetic product, where justified by the nature or the effect of the cosmetic product - Cosmetic product claims substantiation;
5. Data on any animal testing performed by the manufacturer, his agents or suppliers, relating to the development or safety assessment of the cosmetic product or its ingredients, including any animal testing performed to meet the legislative or regulatory requirements of third countries.

Cosmetic Product Safety Report

According to the EU Regulation 1223/2009, the cosmetic product safety report shall, as a minimum, contain the following:

Part A - Cosmetic product safety information:

1) Quantitative and qualitative composition of the cosmetic product;
2) Physical/chemical characteristics and stability of the cosmetic product;
3) Microbiological quality;
4) Impurities, traces, information about the packaging material;
5) Normal and reasonably foreseeable use;
6) Exposure to the cosmetic product;
7) Exposure to the substances;
8) Toxicological profile of the substances;
9) Undesirable effects and serious undesirable effects;
10) Information on the cosmetic product.

Part B - Cosmetic product safety assessment:

1) Assessment conclusion;
2) Labelled warnings and instructions of use;
3) Reasoning (of the scientific excersize);
4) Assessor’s credentials and approval of part B.

Responsible Person

Since the Cosmetics Regulation 1223/2009 entered into force, a Responsible Person (RP) must be designated for each cosmetic product placed on the European market. That person is responsible for ensuring that the product complies with the Cosmetic Regulation. They must also compile the Product Information File and ensure the safety of the product with regards to human health under normal or reasonably foreseeable conditions of use. The Regulation specifies that the RP can be any legal or natural person established within the European Community who will place the cosmetic product on the market and who will therefore act as the unique representative for that product throughout the EU.

The key obligation of the RP is to ensure the safety of the cosmetic product and to ensure the product compliance with the relevant obligations set out in the Cosmetic Regulation. These requirements relate to: cosmetic product safety; good manufacturing practice; safety assessment; product information file; sampling and analysis; product notification; restrictions for certain substances; substances classified as carcinogenic, mutagenic and toxic for reproduction (i.e., CMR substances); nanomaterials; traces of prohibited substances; animal testing; labeling (declaration); product claims; access to information for the public; communication of serious undesirable effects; information on substances.

Duties of the Responsible Person

The Responsible Person is responsible for ensuring that the cosmetic product complies with Regulation 1223/2009 and its amendments. Therefore, the RP plays a key role in marketing the cosmetic products for which he is responsible.The RP has various roles. In particular, RP shell:

• Ensure the safety of the cosmetic product;
• Ensure the compliance of cosmetic product with the applicable requirements of the Regulation;
• Prepare and guarantee the existence and completeness of the PIF for each cosmetic product available on the market;
• Ensure the quality of the manufacturing processes;
• Ensure that the cosmetic product has undergone a safety assessment on the basis of the relevant information by a qualified person and that a CPSR is set up in accordance with Annex I of the Regulation;
• Ensure the compliance with the prohibition on the performance of tests on animals;
• Check the compliance of the product packaging and labelling;
• Carry out the centralized notification of the cosmetic product (Cosmetic Product Notification Portal, CPNP) in its own name;
• Keep and regularly update the PIF in accordance with the new relevant information;
• Make the PIF readily accessible (in electronic or other format) to the competent authorities;
• Be the main point of contact for the competent authorities in the event of an audit.

“A cosmetic product made available on the market shall be safe for human health when used under normal or reasonably foreseeable conditions of use”, … (Regulation EC 1223/2009, Article 3; National Law on General Use Items, Article 63).”

“In order to demonstrate that a cosmetic product complies with the safety requirements, the responsible person shall, prior to placing a cosmetic product on the market, ensure that the cosmetic product has undergone a safety assessment on the basis of the relevant information and that a cosmetic product safety report is set up”, … (Regulation EC 1223/2009, Article 10; National Law on General Use Items, Article 70).”

“When a cosmetic product is placed on the market, the responsible person shall keep and make available a product information file for it”, … (Regulation EC 1223/2009, Article 11; National Law on General Use Items, Article 72).”
