Responsibilities of cosmetic products manufacturers
- 11th of April 2023. is near - Timely action is needed in order to be prepared


Current legislative regarding cosmetic products


Republic of Serbia:

1. Zakon o predmetima opšte upotrebe („Službeni glasnik RS“, br. 25/2019-88);

2. Pravilnik o kozmetičkim proizvodima („Službeni glasnik RS“, br. 60/2019-4).


EU:

1. Regulation (EC) No 1223/2009 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 30 November 2009 on cosmetic products (and its amendments)

11th of April 2023.

All cosmetic products that are on the market in RS, same as for cosmetics in EU, have to comply with certain regulations in order to provide seamless market functioning and buyer’s safety. To this end, on authorities request, manufacturer has to provide Product Information File (PIF), containing, among various data, Cosmetic Product Safety Report (CPSR) as a crucial part that should be an indication of product’s safety.

Since Regulation in Republic of Serbia harmonized with EU 1223/2009 got implemented on 11th of April 2019, grace period of 4 years was allowed for producers to comply with parts of Regulation regarding PIF and CPSR. Namely, according to the Article 117 of the harmonized national Law:

“Subjects in the cosmetics business are obligated to comply with the Articles 70. (Safety Assessment) and 72. (PIF) of this Law at the latest four years after its implementation (11.04.2019.).”

Product Information File

The most important set of data that PIF must contain (please check the exhaustive list of the data set needed for PIF compilation):

1. Clear and unambiguous description of cosmetics product which clearly connects it with the PIF;
2. Cosmetic Product Safety Report;
3. Description of production process and certificate/statement of its compliance with GMP;
4. Cosmetic product claims substantiation;
5. Nanomaterials, CMR substances and animal testing statements.

Cosmetic Product Safety Report

According to the EU 1223/2009, the cosmetic product safety report shall, as a minimum, contain the following:

Part A - Cosmetic product safety information:

1) Quantitative and qualitative composition of the cosmetic product;
2) Physical/chemical characteristics and stability of the cosmetic product;
3) Microbiological quality;
4) Impurities, traces, information about the packaging material;
5) Normal and reasonably foreseeable use;
6) Exposure to the cosmetic product;
7) Exposure to the substances;
8) Toxicological profile of the substances;
9) Undesirable effects and serious undesirable effects;
10) Information on the cosmetic product.

Part B - Cosmetic product safety assessment:

1) Assessment conclusion;
2) Labelled warnings and instructions of use;
3) Reasoning (of the scientific excersize);
4) Assessor’s credentials and approval of part B.

Responsible Person

Since the Cosmetics Regulation 1223/2009 entered into force, a Responsible Person (RP) must be designated for each cosmetic product placed on the European market. That person is responsible for ensuring that the product complies with the Cosmetic Regulation. They must also compile the Product Information File and ensure the safety of the product with regards to human health under normal or reasonably foreseeable conditions of use.

The Regulation specifies that the RP can be any natural or legal person established within the European Community who will act as the unique representative for that product throughout the EU. The RP’s role is to ensure that every product placed on the market complies with the Cosmetic Regulation.

Duties of the Responsible Person

The Responsible Person is responsible for ensuring that the cosmetic product complies with Regulation 1223/2009 and its amendments. Therefore, RP plays a key role in marketing the cosmetic products for which they are responsible.The RP has various roles. In particular, RP must:

• Ensure the safety of the cosmetic product;
• Guarantee the existence and completeness of the Production Information File for any cosmetic product placed on the European market;
• Ensure the quality of the manufacturing processes;
• Guarantee compliance of the cosmetic formula;
• Ensure compliance with the prohibition on the performance of tests on animals;
• Check the compliance of the product packaging and labelling;
• Carry out the CPNP notification in their own name;
• Be the main point of contact for the competent authorities in the event of an audit.
